Short Bio

I am a Ph.D. graduate in Computer Science at the University of New South Wales (UNSW Sydney), Australia where I was a member of Networked Systems and Security Group (NetSys) at the School of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE). During my PhD studies, I was also affiliated with information Security and Privacy Group at Data61 | CSIRO. Prior commencing my PhD, I was a research assistant at Software Engineering Laboratory (SElab) in the Department of Information Technologies, at Åbo Akademi University, Finland. I received my M.Sc. in Information Technology specialising in Electronics and Communication systems from the University of Turku, Finland. Before moving into research, I worked more than 10 years in telecommunication industry where I developed various telephony signalling protocols such as SS7 and V5 interface.

My research focuses on Cyber-physical Systems/Internet of Things (IoT) Security and Computer Networks. Specifically, my research interests spans from Adversarial Machine Learning and Data Security to adopting Software Defined-networking (SDN) and Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT) to address security and privacy concerns in IoT systems.
